Crafting Stories for the Village
Triangulation Process and Learning Plans
To make students learn how to craft their stories, they need to be told some well-crafted stories.

The teaching-learning process at the Village is learning-oriented and not teaching-oriented. It has three components - goals, assessment, and activities. It is imperative to think of them as complementary to each other and not separate.

The Triangulation Process serves as a tool that compliments this pedagogical model. More practically, it is the process that faculty at TDV uses to create effective learning plans.
Structure of Learning Plans
The structuring of a learning plan is split into three stages - learning goals, learning assessment, and learning activities. At the crux of this process is the idea of working backwards while creating learning plans so that the course is not dependent on what the teacher has to teach but what are the desired goals from a particular course - identifying desired goals comes first and learning teaching activities last.
