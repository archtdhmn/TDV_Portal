Assessment Policy
The journey of each Villager is unique in its own right, it carries a personal story within. The academic evaluation at TDV is based on this personal story, their journey. The criteria have been deliberately designed to be multi-dimensional and not unilateral. The larger goal is for the assessment tools to be able to incorporate a larger spectrum of designers- thinkers- doers- makers. This is essentially where the four pillars of TDV - Courage, Perseverance, Awareness and Compassion come to life as the parameters of evaluation.

Parameters of Evaluation
'Courage', 'perseverance', 'awareness', 'compassion' are all ideas we hate to put into a box. Within the assessment policy, these parameters of evaluation are never concretely defined but explored.

Hover over these words to see some manifestations and ask yourself, 'what do these words mean to me?'.
Courage
Pushing one's self as an individual.
Perhaps the experimentation of ideas and testing the concepts?
Sometimes the innovativeness in research methodology.
Perseverance
Extensive exploration of brief, materials, domains.
Maybe the rigour of process, attempts and repetition?
Is it the drive and ability to carry an idea forward?
Awareness
Perhaps the thoroughness of research, analysis and synthesis?
Maybe it is the exploration of complexities.
Compassion
Audience understanding, insights, response to contemporary issues.
Or is it the drive to be themselves?
